Frequently Asked Questions

Is PassGen safe to use?

Yes, PassGen is completely safe. All password generation happens directly in your browser using the Web Crypto API (crypto.getRandomValues()), which provides cryptographically secure random values. Your passwords are never transmitted over the internet or stored anywhere.

What makes a strong password?

A strong password typically has:

  • At least 12-16 characters in length
  • A mix of uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Numbers and special symbols
  • No personal information or common words
  • True randomness (not human-generated patterns)
How does PassGen generate passwords?

PassGen uses your browser's built-in cryptographic random number generator (crypto.getRandomValues()). This is the same technology used by banks and security applications. Each character is selected completely at random from the character set you choose, ensuring maximum unpredictability.

Are my passwords stored anywhere?

No. PassGen never stores, transmits, or logs your generated passwords. The application runs entirely in your browser with no backend server. Once you leave the page, your generated passwords are gone forever unless you've copied them.

How long should my password be?

We recommend at least 16 characters for most accounts. For high-security accounts (banking, email, password managers), consider 20+ characters. The password strength meter will help you understand how secure your chosen length is against brute-force attacks.
